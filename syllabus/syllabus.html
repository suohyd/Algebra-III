    Course Description
    🔗

    🔗
    Course Designations and Attributes
    🔗
    Breadth
    🔗

    🔗
    Level
    🔗
    M.Sc.
    🔗
    🔗
    Requisite
    🔗
    Students should have basic knowledge of group theory (Algebra-I course) and ring theory (Algebra-II course).
    🔗
    Instructional modality
    🔗
    In-person
    🔗
    🔗

    Learning Outcomes.

    1. Define various notions from field extensions, for example algebraic, transcendental, normal separable, the degree of an extension, etc.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Identify and apply the above notions to various examples.
      🔗
      🔗
    3. State and understand the Galois correspondence, and analyze some examples.
      🔗
      🔗
    4. Understand how to apply the Galois correspondence to other areas in mathematics, like classical or algebraic geometry, or in commutative algebra.
      🔗
      🔗
